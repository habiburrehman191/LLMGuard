The travel expense policy governs airfare, lodging, meals, ground transport, and related costs for approved business travel.
Employees should book economy airfare and standard rail fares through the corporate travel platform unless a medical need or client requirement is documented.
Hotel bookings should stay within the city rate cap published by Procurement unless conference rates or safety considerations justify an exception.
Travelers must keep itemized receipts for flights, hotels, taxis, parking, and any single meal expense above 25 dollars.
Expense reports should be submitted within 10 business days after returning from the trip so Finance can reconcile card activity quickly.
Personal detours, minibar charges, entertainment, and upgrades unrelated to business needs are not reimbursable.
Managers should review trip costs for policy compliance before Finance completes the final audit.
Employees who lose a receipt must provide a written explanation and may be asked for an alternative proof of payment.
