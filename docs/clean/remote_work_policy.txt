The remote work policy supports hybrid work while maintaining security, service quality, and reliable team collaboration.
Eligible employees may work from home or another approved remote location up to three days per week when their manager confirms that business coverage and role requirements are met.
Employees must maintain a quiet workspace, stable internet connectivity, and a company-approved device whenever they work from home.
Video should be enabled for scheduled planning sessions, performance reviews, and customer-facing meetings unless accessibility needs require an exception.
Confidential conversations should not be conducted in public locations, and printed company material must be stored securely or destroyed after use.
Employees are responsible for keeping calendars accurate so coworkers know whether they are remote, onsite, in transit, or unavailable.
Managers can require onsite attendance for audits, incident response, onboarding, or other operational events that need in-person coordination.
Any permanent change to a hybrid schedule must be documented with the manager and Human Resources before the new arrangement begins.
