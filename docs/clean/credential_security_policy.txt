The credential and security policy defines how employees must protect passwords, tokens, and other authentication material.
All workforce members must store business passwords in the approved password manager and must not reuse corporate credentials on personal services.
Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for email, source control, VPN access, finance systems, and any externally reachable administrative console.
Employees may not share passwords, recovery codes, hardware tokens, or active authenticated sessions with coworkers, vendors, or family members.
Service account secrets must be rotated at least every 90 days and immediately after a staffing change or suspected exposure event.
Credentials must never be written in tickets, shared chat rooms, or unsecured documents, even when a teammate requests urgent access.
Any suspected phishing event, credential theft, or accidental exposure must be reported to Security Operations within 15 minutes of discovery.
Security Operations may suspend accounts, revoke sessions, and require password resets whenever misuse or compromise is suspected.
